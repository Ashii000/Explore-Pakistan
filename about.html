About Pakistan

History, Culture & Modern Nation

Welcome to Pakistan

Pakistan is a country in South Asia, created in 1947. It is rich in culture, languages, and diverse landscapes—from Himalayan mountains to Arabian Sea coastlines. Pakistan is a land of breathtaking landscapes, rich history, and warm hospitality. From the snow-capped peaks of the Karakoram in the north to the golden deserts of Thar in the south, the country offers unmatched natural beauty.

Visitors can explore ancient civilizations like Mohenjo-Daro, marvel at Mughal-era architecture in Lahore, trek through the world's most spectacular mountain ranges, or enjoy the vibrant bazaars and delicious street food. What truly sets Pakistan apart, however, is its people — known for their friendliness and generosity, they welcome travelers like family.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Islamabad
  • Population: 240 million (approx.)
  • Area: 881,913 km²
  • Provinces: Punjab, Sindh, KP, Balochistan, Gilgit‑Baltistan
  • National Language: Urdu

Key Milestones in History

1947 – Independence from British rule

Pakistan emerged as an independent nation on August 14, 1947, under the leadership of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah.

1956 – Islamic Republic

Pakistan adopted its first constitution, officially becoming the Islamic Republic of Pakistan.

1998 – Nuclear Power

Pakistan conducted successful nuclear tests in Chagai, Balochistan, becoming the world's seventh nuclear power.

2015 – CPEC Launch

The China-Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC) was initiated, marking a new era of infrastructure and economic development.

Pakistan Today

Pakistan continues to evolve as a resilient nation. It has played a vital role in regional politics and global peacekeeping. The country is focusing on digital transformation, youth empowerment, and economic stability. With a rich cultural heritage, natural resources, and a strong sense of identity, Pakistan stands as an important country in South Asia.
