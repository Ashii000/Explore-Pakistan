Welcome to Sindh

Land of Indus Civilization and Cultural Diversity

Introduction

Sindh is located in the southeastern region of Pakistan and is one of the four provinces of the country. Known for its deep-rooted history, Sindh is the cradle of the ancient Indus Valley Civilization.

The people of Sindh are known for their hospitality, traditional Ajrak clothing, Sufi music, and rich cultural values that have been preserved for centuries.

Historical Significance

Sindh has been home to ancient civilizations, Islamic empires, and colonial rulers, each leaving their mark on the region's culture and architecture.

Historical Importance

Mohenjo-Daro

One of the earliest known urban settlements in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage site dating back to 2500 BCE.

Sufi Heritage

Major Cities

Karachi

The largest city, financial capital, and home to Pakistan's busiest port.

Hyderabad

Known for its bangles, history, and delicious Sindhi cuisine.

Sukkur

Famous for its barrage, dates, and as a gateway to Upper Sindh.

Thatta

Historical city with Makli Necropolis and Shah Jahan Mosque.

Culture & Traditions

Ajrak & Sindhi Topi

Traditional attire includes the Ajrak (block-printed shawl) and Sindhi Topi (cap), both considered symbols of honor and cultural identity.

Sufi Music

Sindhi music is deeply rooted in Sufi poetry, with instruments like the Yaktaro and Chapri creating mesmerizing melodies.

Sindhi Cultural Day

Annual celebrations are held with great zeal across the province and by the Sindhi diaspora worldwide.
